Edouard as a journalist and press correspondent

My research has identified that Edouard wrote regularly for the Daily Mail between 1926 and 1933 and was attributed his own byline for these articles. He also wrote a number of letters to the Editor of the Daily Mail during this period. A number of articles have also been found in other newspapers across the globe. The subjects covered included Russian society, poverty, the Bolsheviks, Russian leaders and a number of travelogues - showing his roots as a socio-political specialist, an economic commentator and an expert on economic trade. I have now obtained copies of these articles from the Daily Mail archives and through the good offices of Suzanne Barnes these have been typed up and as far as possible we have attempted to identify certain words and phrases which have been difficult to read. The table below provides a list of the various articles and letters to the Editor of the Daily Mail – an eclectic collection – with my own comments and observations:

01/10/1926 “Young Reds at Work” Growth of ‘hooliganism’ particularly amongst the League of Communist Youth.

29/03/1927 “Radek’s hand in Shanghai” Reporting the delight of the Red leaders at Moscow from the revolution taking place in China and particularly the reign of terror at Shanghai.

27/06/1927 “How the Reds ruin Children” Relating the story of 6 British children travelling to Russia, in defiance of the ban by the British Government, to spend time with the “Pioneer Movement” – a pseudo-Scout organization formed by the Soviets in 1922.
08/09/1927 “Spy Mad” New approach by the Soviets starting in 1924 to arrest people who had come in contact with foreigners, chiefly British – accusing them of spying in Russia on behalf of the British.
14/09/1927 “Judges and Hangman Too” Highlighting the fact that many Soviet judges are not actually qualified for the role – education , training and experience found wanting – not even always qualified lawyers!! – supports the fact that Edouard is reported as being a Barrister
01/11/1927 “4 Years in a Desert” Reporting the discovery of Khara-Khoto, the former capital of the Si-sia race led by Ghenghiz Khan.
15/12/1927 “Trotsky’s Fight for life” “Edouard – a leading authority on Russia who knows the chief figures in the dramatic struggle for power no proceeding”

01/03/1928 “Hungary Crowds of Moscow” The length of Moscow queues – the housewife now has to wait 6 hours a day – prices are 150% above their pre-war levels – the Russian worker lives on one quarter of the British standard of living!
13/03/1928 “Kabul” Description by Edouard of a trip to Kabul – it states “before the war” – so before 1914.
It also highlights the fact he had also visited Persia as he compares Kabul with Teheran.
Finally, he states that although the rest of the Mission team stayed in a hotel he actually “personally enjoyed the hospitality of a wealthy citizen and was lavishly entertained in his semi-oriental palace”!

11/07/1928 “Farce of Soviet Justice” Edouard is described as “A Russian Barrister” and he certainly looks the part when describing the trial of 50 Russian engineers – “I have before me the verbatim report of the trial and a large number of articles and comments upon the case”.
31/08/1928 “Elephant Racing in Afghanistan” Outlines Edouard’s visit to Kabul – describing the various festivities which were taking place
12/09/1928 “On The Pamir mountain expedition and its dangers” Looking for a lost people in the Pamir mountains
02/10/1928 “Revolt of Russia’s Youth” Children of the Revolution are finding life dull and their restless spirits are now turning towards a revolution of their own making!
06/10/1928 “Beautiful Red Spies” Edouard reports that he came across many beautiful women spies in “the difficult years of 1905 and 1906” – where was this, at the time of the 1905 Revolution ?!
The most beautiful are delegated to watch foreign visitors – women spies are in the majority – lured by high salaries.
21/12/1928 “Reds Cannot Kill Christmas” Christmas is regarded as the most serious crime against the Dictatorship of the Proletariat but the Kremlin fails to stop Russians enjoying the Christmas period.
XX/04/1929 Article written by [XXX] entitled “Exports to Russia” Refers to the “Can we trade with Russia” pamphlet written by Edouard.
…a very clever and timely suggestion….. his suggestion ought to receive the immediate attention of Government and of every business man … Dr Luboff gives a masterly analysis of Soviet trade conditions…
23/09/1929 “The Reds grow Comic – but it’s not so funny for the Russian people” Life in Soviet Russia is fought on ‘fronts’ – the potato front, the haircut front, the queue front, the boot mending front ….. !!!
Hilarious – Pravda says that 15m people cannot have their boots mended this year…… does the swine front mean the conscription of pigs ?!
Leads to the Queue Brigades to patrol the queues – well-well!
23/09/1929 Letter to the Editor: “Real value of wages” The wages of miners are now so low they leave the pits to work as agricultural laborers.
25/04/1930 “Moscow’s Godless” … an organization formed by the Kremlin as a body for “the final destruction of every vestige of religion”
16/03/1931 “Dumping must be fought” Comment on Edouard’s “masterly pamphlet, Soviet Dumping – the facts which it contains are beyond dispute.
16/06/1932 “What the Russian Five Year Plan has achieved” Suggests significant progress has been made in the first 2 ½ years of the Plan but warns that the bulk of the necessary finance has come from foreigners – a confusing positive article which requires further research !
XX/XX/1932 “Soviet Oil figures” Notes that Edouard “reports in his Russian trade news service” that Soviet oil output has in fact been falling.
24/02/1933 Letter to Ed from 40 Elizabeth Street – “Re: nervous breakdown” The letter praises an article by Dr Beron Wolfe on factors and treatments for nervous breakdowns. Interestingly he starts by saying Those who have suffered the agonies of a nervous breakdown… This strongly suggests that he himself had suffered from a nervous breakdown !!
17/05/1933 “Glance at Russian Diplomacy” Attributed as “Former Member of the Russian Bar”
Russia claims that it is opposed to all international combinations BUT has signed a Gernan-Russian Treaty and has ‘understandings’ with France and Poland – mainly to play them off against GB!
25/05/1933 “Red Russia’s 50,000,000 Capitalists” Focuses on the structuring of internal loans by the Soviet authorities to finance the second Five-Year Plan. – really a form of financial capitalism with 50 million Russian people forecast to draw dividends under these loans !!
18/04/1933 Soviet Judges and their Verdicts” Damming commentary on the Russian justice and trial system – no jury involved – closed door process with few acquittals for Russians and foreigners normally ending up being sent back to their home countries.
23/05/1933 “Stalin’s Sabotage Bogy” Sabotage a much beloved word in Moscow – used to frighten people into submission – people are accused of sabotaging the Soviet state through their actions or failure to deliver on target and on time !
11/04/1933 “Taint of Politics in Soviet Justice” Edouard – described as a former member of the Russian Bar – describes the very significant differences between the British system of jurisprudence and the Soviet system!
In Russia:
  • prisoners are generally condemned beforehand
  • there is no equality before the law for everyone
  • the State is supreme and so no independent judicial body
  • forced interrogations take place
  • there is no jury
  • the defence cannot challenge very point in the prosecution’s case
  • arrest and sentencing is carried out by the OGPU
  • the Public Procurator acts as the Judge assisted by the OGPU

In essence “Soviet law is frankly biased and quite openly chained to the chariot of the Class State”.
